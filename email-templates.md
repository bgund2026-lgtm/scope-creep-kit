# Email Templates for Freelancers

**10 copy-paste templates for every scope-related situation. Personalize the brackets, hit send.**

---

## Template 1: Project Kickoff — Setting Boundaries Early

> Subject: Let's get started! [Project Name] kickoff details
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> I'm excited to kick off [Project Name]! Before we dive in, I want to share a few details that'll help everything run smoothly:
>
> **What happens next:**
> 1. I'll send the initial [wireframes / first draft / concepts] by [date]
> 2. You'll have [X] business days to review and provide feedback
> 3. We'll move through [X] revision rounds per phase, then advance to the next milestone
>
> **A quick note on how I work:**
> - All deliverables and timelines are outlined in our SOW — if anything comes up that's outside that scope, I'll send a change order with pricing before starting any additional work
> - For feedback, I ask that all comments come through [one point of contact / your designated reviewer] to keep things clear and efficient
> - If you need anything outside our agreed scope, just ask! I'm happy to help — I'll just provide a quote first so there are no surprises
>
> **What I need from you to get started:**
> - [Asset/copy/access item 1] by [date]
> - [Asset/copy/access item 2] by [date]
>
> Looking forward to building something great together.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

## Template 2: When the Client Asks for "Just One Small Thing"

> Subject: Re: [Their email subject]
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> Thanks for the idea — [brief acknowledgment of what they want, e.g., "adding a testimonial carousel could definitely enhance the homepage"].
>
> This falls outside our current scope, so I'd need to add it as a change order. Here's a quick breakdown:
>
> - **What's involved:** [Brief description, e.g., "Design and develop a 3-card testimonial carousel with auto-scroll and swipe functionality"]
> - **Estimated time:** [X] hours
> - **Cost:** $[X]
> - **Timeline impact:** [+X business days]
>
> If you'd like to move forward, I'll send over a change order for your approval. Otherwise, we'll stick with our current plan and keep things on track.
>
> Let me know what you'd prefer!
>
> [Your Name]

## Template 3: When the Client Says "This Should Be Quick"

> Subject: Re: [Their email subject]
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> I hear you — it can seem like a quick change from the outside! Here's what's actually involved:
>
> [Break down the work honestly, e.g., "Updating the navigation structure means reflowing the header across all 8 pages, retesting the mobile menu, updating the CSS, and checking for any broken links — that's about 3 hours of work including QA."]
>
> I want to make sure it's done right rather than rushed. The cost would be $[X] as a change order. Would you like me to proceed, or should we keep things as-is for now?
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

## Template 4: When the Client References a Past Freelancer

> Subject: Re: [Their email subject]
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> I understand it can feel surprising when something isn't included — especially if you've had a different experience before.
>
> I can't speak to how other freelancers structure their pricing, but my approach is to keep my base rates competitive by pricing the specific scope we agreed on. This way, you're not overpaying for things you don't need, and any additions are priced transparently.
>
> I'd be happy to add [the requested work] as a change order for $[X]. I always want to be upfront about costs so there are no surprises on either side.
>
> Let me know if you'd like to proceed, or if you'd rather keep our current scope as-is.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

## Template 5: Change Order Presentation

> Subject: Change Order #[00X] — [Project Name]
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> As we discussed, I've put together a change order for [the additional work]:
>
> **Change Order #00X**
>
> | Description | Hours | Rate | Total |
> |------------|-------|------|-------|
> | [Task 1] | [X] | $[X]/hr | $[X] |
> | [Task 2] | [X] | $[X]/hr | $[X] |
> | [Task 3] | [X] | $[X]/hr | $[X] |
>
> **Total additional cost:** $[X]
> **Timeline adjustment:** [+X business days]
> **New project total:** $[original + change order]
>
> Once you review and sign the attached change order, I can get started on this right away. If you'd like to adjust the scope (e.g., do a simpler version), I'm happy to revise the estimate.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
>
> **Attachment:** [Change Order Form - CO-00X.pdf]

## Template 7: Project Completion — Prevents Post-Project Creep

> Subject: [Project Name] is complete! 🎉
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> I'm happy to report that [Project Name] is complete! Here's a quick summary of what was delivered:
>
> **Deliverables:**
> - [Deliverable 1]
> - [Deliverable 2]
> - [Deliverable 3]
>
> **All scope items from our original SOW (and [X] change orders) have been fulfilled.**
>
> **What happens next:**
> - [Any post-launch support period, e.g., "I'm available for bug fixes for 14 days after launch"]
> - After [date], any additional work will be quoted as a new project or hourly engagement
>
> **A few things to keep in mind going forward:**
> - [Maintenance tips, e.g., "Keep WordPress and plugins updated monthly"]
> - [Renewal reminders, e.g., "Your domain renews on [date] — make sure payment info is current"]
>
> It's been great working with you on this. If you need anything in the future — whether it's updates, new features, or a completely new project — I'd love to help. Just reach out!
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

## Template 8: Revision Limit Notification

> Subject: Quick update — revision rounds for [Phase Name]
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> Just a heads-up that we've used [X of Y] included revision rounds for [Phase Name]. [One more round remains / We've used all included rounds.]
>
> [If one more remains:] "You have one more revision round included in our agreement. After that, additional revisions are billed at $[X]/hour."
>
> [If all are used:] "Going forward, additional revisions are $[X]/hour, billed in 15-minute increments. I'm happy to keep refining — I just want to make sure we're on the same page about the cost."
>
> Let me know how you'd like to proceed!
>
> [Your Name]

## Template 9: "I Value Our Relationship, But..." — Boundary Email

> Subject: Quick note about our project scope
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> I want to address something because I value our working relationship and want to make sure we stay on the same page.
>
> Over the last [time period], I've received several requests beyond our original scope — including [list 2-3 examples]. I've been happy to accommodate some of these, but I'm reaching a point where continuing to add work without adjusting the scope isn't sustainable for me.
>
> I want to deliver excellent work on schedule, and that means being realistic about what fits within our current agreement. Going forward, I'll be processing any out-of-scope requests through change orders. This isn't about being difficult — it's about making sure I can give every part of your project the attention it deserves without burning out or falling behind.
>
> I'm still fully committed to delivering everything in our original scope, and I'm excited about what we're building together. If you'd like to discuss expanding the project scope, I'm happy to put together options at different price points.
>
> Thanks for understanding,
>
> [Your Name]

## Template 10: New Project Proposal — References Completed Scope Well

> Subject: Proposal: [New Project Name]
>
> Hi [Client Name],
>
> It was great working together on [Previous Project Name] — I hope [specific result, e.g., "the new site is driving those appointment bookings you wanted"].
>
> I'd love to help with [what they mentioned / what you noticed they need]. I've put together a proposal:
>
> **[New Project Name]**
>
> **Scope:**
> - [Deliverable 1]
> - [Deliverable 2]
> - [Deliverable 3]
>
> **Timeline:** [X] weeks
> **Investment:** $[X]
>
> **What's NOT included this time** (learning from last project!):
> - [Item 1, e.g., "Copywriting — same as before, you'll provide final copy"]
> - [Item 2, e.g., "Photography"]
>
> Same process as before: detailed SOW, milestone payments, change orders for any additions. It worked well last time, so let's stick with what works.
>
> Would you like to set up a quick call to discuss? I have some ideas based on how [Previous Project] performed that I think you'll find interesting.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
