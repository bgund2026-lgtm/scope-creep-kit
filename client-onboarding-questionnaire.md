## For the Freelancer: How to Use This Questionnaire

### Before the Proposal
Send this questionnaire after your initial call/meeting but before you write the proposal. Their answers will tell you:
- Whether they know what they want (or need you to help them figure it out — charge for that)
- Whether their budget aligns with their expectations
- How many decision-makers will be in the room (more = more revisions = price accordingly)
- Whether they're organized enough to be a good client

### Red Flags to Watch For
- **"Not sure yet" on more than 3 answers** — They're not ready. Propose a discovery/strategy phase first.
- **"We'll decide as a team"** — No single decision-maker. Add 30-50% more revision rounds to your pricing.
- **Content plan is "not sure yet"** — Build a content strategy add-on into your proposal, or accept that your timeline will slip.
- **Budget is "prefer not to say"** — They either can't afford you or are hoping you'll lowball yourself. Give them your real price.
- **Previous freelancer "had issues"** — Find out what kind of issues. Were they slow? Did they ghost? Or did the client scope-creep them and the freelancer pushed back?

### After They Return It
1. Read it thoroughly. Highlight gaps.
2. Schedule a 30-minute clarification call if anything is unclear.
3. Use their answers to write a proposal that matches their expectations — and explicitly calls out what's NOT included based on their "nice to have" list.
4. The questionnaire becomes the foundation of your SOW. Reference it directly: "As outlined in your onboarding questionnaire dated [date], the following deliverables are in scope..."
