## How to Present a Change Order Professionally

### The Right Mindset

Change orders aren't punishments. They're how professionals handle the reality that projects evolve. Your client isn't doing something wrong by requesting additional work — they're just asking for more than what they paid for. The change order simply says: "Great idea, here's what that costs."

### Presentation Tips

**1. Deliver change orders promptly — within 24-48 hours of the request.**
Delays make it feel like you're nickel-and-diming. Speed makes it feel like professional project management.

**2. Present it as a solution, not a roadblock.**
- ❌ "That's out of scope, so I'd need to charge you."
- ✅ "Great addition — here's what that looks like in terms of time and cost."

**3. Frame the cost against the value.**
Don't just say "it'll cost $1,000 more." Say: "Adding the Team page gives your visitors confidence in your staff's expertise. It's $1,080 additional and adds 5 business days to the timeline."

**4. Use a consistent process every time.**
When every addition gets the same professional treatment, it becomes expected — not confrontational. The form does the heavy lifting so you don't have to.

**5. Never apologize for the price.**
The price is what the work costs. You're not being difficult — you're being professional. If you apologize, you signal that the price isn't fair. It is.

**6. Offer options when possible.**
"I can add the full Team page with individual bios for $1,080, or we could do a simpler team section on the About page for $400. Which works better for you?"

**7. Follow up if it goes unsigned.**
If 48 hours pass without a signature, send a friendly nudge: "Just checking — are we moving forward with CO-001? I want to make sure we keep the timeline on track."

### Email Script for Sending a Change Order

> Hi [Client Name],
>
> Thanks for mentioning [the new request] — I think it'll be a great addition to the project.
>
> I've put together Change Order #001 outlining the additional work, cost, and timeline impact. You'll find it attached. Once you review and sign, I can get started on it right away.
>
> Let me know if you have any questions or want to discuss any of the details.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
